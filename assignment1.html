Assignment 1: Confounding Tools

Brief Outline

"Design a 24” × 36” digital poster about either 1) your life under COVID 19 or 2) how you see the future of design *Without using software explicitly intended for design.*

Option 1) Some aspect of your life under COVID19. How has it changed? What have you lost contact with? What have you gained contact with? Think of ways to represent your life under current conditions." -GRPH 2010-005

My Approach

I chose to go the direction of life under/with covid, and how so much has been happening in the world that it can be overwhelming. But at the same time I wanting to depict the feeling of slowly becoming numb to big news as well because of this reason. Therefore my goal was to create a choatic poster with multiple news articles headlines. This process was very time consuming and made me remember a lot of events from the last two years that I forgot. I experimented with both pages and my desktop. The pages version is my favourite, as it has the most common words watermarked overtop of the news articles, adding to the chaos. I attempting to do the same thing with the desktop version but it appeared out of place.

I was really struggling with this assignment. I couldn’t come up with a solid idea and when I did have an idea I couldn’t illustrate the idea the way I wanted to. There was also a lot of external factors that made this project really hard me. I was not satisfied with my final product but I just wanted to be done with the project. So I simply tweaked the project based on critiques, and called it done.

First Iteration

In this iteration I was really set on using screen shots of various news article title and putting them all together in Pages. More process pieces were made for this iteration but never finished as I did not believe they looked good. For example I had started a piece where I would layer the news articles on top of each other with varying opacities. However it became a jumbled mess of type and colour rather quickly, so I trashed it instead of continuing it.
